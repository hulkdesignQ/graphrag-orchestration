# CONSULTING SERVICES AGREEMENT

**Agreement Number:** CSA-2026-0418  
**Effective Date:** January 15, 2026

---

## PARTIES

This Consulting Services Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into by and between:

**Client:** Meridian Health Systems, Inc., a Delaware corporation with its principal office at 1200 Harbor Boulevard, Suite 400, San Francisco, CA 94111 ("Client")

**Consultant:** NovaTech Solutions Pty Ltd, an Australian proprietary company with its registered office at Level 12, 88 Collins Street, Melbourne, VIC 3000, Australia ("Consultant")

---

## 1. SCOPE OF SERVICES

The Consultant shall provide the following services ("Services"):

1.1 **System Architecture Review** — A comprehensive review of the Client's existing healthcare data infrastructure, including all electronic health record (EHR) systems, patient management platforms, and interoperability layers.

1.2 **Cloud Migration Strategy** — Development of a phased migration plan to transition the Client's on-premises systems to a hybrid cloud environment using Microsoft Azure, with an estimated timeline of 18 months.

1.3 **Compliance Assessment** — Evaluation of the Client's current HIPAA compliance posture, identification of gaps, and delivery of a remediation roadmap within 60 days of project commencement.

1.4 **Staff Training** — Delivery of up to 40 hours of hands-on training for the Client's IT staff on cloud-native tools, DevOps practices, and security monitoring procedures.

---

## 2. COMPENSATION AND PAYMENT TERMS

2.1 **Fixed Fee.** The total fee for the Services described in Section 1 shall be **USD $485,000** (Four Hundred Eighty-Five Thousand US Dollars), payable in the following installments:

| Milestone | Amount | Due Date |
|-----------|--------|----------|
| Project Kickoff | $97,000 (20%) | Upon execution of this Agreement |
| Architecture Review Completion | $121,250 (25%) | April 30, 2026 |
| Migration Plan Delivery | $121,250 (25%) | July 31, 2026 |
| Final Delivery & Training | $145,500 (30%) | October 31, 2026 |

2.2 **Expense Reimbursement.** The Client shall reimburse the Consultant for all pre-approved travel and accommodation expenses incurred in connection with the Services. Business-class airfare is authorized for international travel exceeding 8 hours. Total reimbursable expenses shall not exceed **$35,000** without prior written approval.

2.3 **Late Payment.** Any payment not received within 30 days of the invoice date shall accrue interest at a rate of 1.5% per month, compounded monthly, until paid in full.

2.4 **Currency.** All amounts are in United States Dollars. Payments shall be made by wire transfer to the account designated by the Consultant.

---

## 3. TERM AND TERMINATION

3.1 **Term.** This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and continue until December 31, 2026, unless terminated earlier in accordance with this Section.

3.2 **Termination for Convenience.** Either party may terminate this Agreement upon 30 days' written notice to the other party. In the event of such termination, the Client shall pay the Consultant for all Services performed and expenses incurred through the date of termination.

3.3 **Termination for Cause.** Either party may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice if the other party materially breaches any provision of this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within 15 business days after receiving written notice thereof.

3.4 **Effect of Termination.** Upon termination, the Consultant shall deliver to the Client all work product completed as of the termination date. Sections 4, 5, 6, and 8 shall survive termination.

---

## 4. CONFIDENTIALITY

4.1 **Definition.** "Confidential Information" means all non-public information disclosed by one party to the other, whether orally, in writing, or electronically, that is designated as confidential or that reasonably should be understood to be confidential given the nature of the information and circumstances of disclosure. This includes, without limitation, patient health information (PHI), system architecture diagrams, security protocols, financial data, and business strategies.

4.2 **Obligations.** The receiving party shall: (a) use Confidential Information solely for the purpose of performing its obligations under this Agreement; (b) protect Confidential Information using the same degree of care it uses to protect its own confidential information, but no less than reasonable care; and (c) not disclose Confidential Information to any third party without the prior written consent of the disclosing party.

4.3 **Duration.** The obligations of confidentiality shall survive termination of this Agreement for a period of five (5) years, except with respect to trade secrets, which shall be protected indefinitely.

To the extent any pre-existing IP is incorporated into the Work Product, the Consultant grants the Client a non-exclusive, perpetual, royalty-free license to use such pre-existing IP solely in connection with the Work Product.

5.3 **Feedback.** Any suggestions or feedback provided by the Client regarding the Consultant's tools or methodologies shall not create any obligation or intellectual property right.

## 6. LIABILITY AND INDEMNIFICATION

6.1 **Limitation of Liability.** In no event shall either party's total aggregate liability under this Agreement exceed the total fees paid or payable under this Agreement. Neither party shall be liable for any indirect, incidental, special, consequential, or punitive damages.

6.2 **Indemnification.** The Consultant shall indemnify, defend, and hold harmless the Client from any third-party claims arising from: (a) the Consultant's gross negligence or willful misconduct; (b) infringement of any third-party intellectual property rights by the Work Product; or (c) the Consultant's breach of confidentiality obligations.

6.3 **Insurance.** The Consultant shall maintain professional liability insurance with coverage of no less than **$2,000,000** per occurrence and **$5,000,000** in the aggregate throughout the term of this Agreement and for 24 months thereafter.

## 7. DATA PROTECTION

7.1 **HIPAA Compliance.** The parties acknowledge that the Consultant may have access to Protected Health Information (PHI) as defined under HIPAA. The Consultant agrees to enter into a separate Business Associate Agreement (BAA) prior to accessing any PHI.

7.2 **Data Security.** The Consultant shall implement and maintain administrative, physical, and technical safeguards consistent with industry standards (including SOC 2 Type II and ISO 27001) to protect all Client data.

7.3 **Breach Notification.** The Consultant shall notify the Client within 48 hours of discovering any actual or suspected security breach involving Client data.
