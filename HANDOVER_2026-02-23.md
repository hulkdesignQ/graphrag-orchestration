## Remaining Issues — Prioritized TODO List

### Priority 1: Q-G6 Role Assignment Inversion

**Problem:** The entity-doc map correctly lists 4 entities with per-document [PARTY_TO] / [---] tags and sentence context. But the synthesis LLM inverts roles — says Contoso is "Buyer/Owner" when it should be "Owner" only in PMA/HTC, and says Fabrikam "signs on behalf of Walt Flood Realty" when Fabrikam is actually Builder/Pumper/Customer.

**Root cause:** The sentence context shows preamble text like `(hereinafter "Builder")` or `(hereinafter "Owner")`, but the LLM doesn't correctly associate the role designation with the entity when composing the final answer.

**Potential fixes:**
- **(A) Extract explicit role designation**: Parse the preamble role (e.g., "Builder", "Owner", "Customer") from the sentence context and add it as a separate field in the entity-doc map. Change `[PARTY_TO]` to `[PARTY_TO: Builder]` or `[PARTY_TO: Owner]`.
- **(B) Indexing-time role extraction**: During graph construction, extract party roles from agreement preambles and store as properties on Entity nodes or RELATED_TO edges (e.g., `party_role: "Builder"`).
- **(C) Synthesis constraint refinement**: Add an instruction telling the LLM to quote the exact role designation from the sentence context rather than inferring it.

**Recommendation:** Start with (A) — regex extraction of `(hereinafter "...")` or `(the "...")` patterns from the sentence context at query time. Low risk, no indexing changes needed.

### Priority 2: Q-G4 Synthesis Over-Inclusion

**Problem:** The LLM finds both ground-truth reporting obligations (holding tank pumper reports + PMA monthly statement) but also incorrectly classifies many other clauses as "reporting/record-keeping" (filing contracts with county, warranty claim letters, arbitration awards, etc.).

**Root cause:** Synthesis LLM interprets "reporting/record-keeping" too broadly. This is the same category of issue as the previous Q-G7 problem (over-generation) which was fixed for Q-G7 by the per-document entity-doc map, but Q-G4 doesn't use the entity-doc map (it's not an entity enumeration query).

**Potential fixes:**
- **(A) Query-type-specific synthesis hints**: The existing `reporting_hint` in synthesis.py instructs "enumerate distinct reporting/record-keeping obligations as bullet points" but doesn't constrain what counts. Add scoping: "Only include obligations explicitly described as reports, statements, or record-keeping — not general contractual requirements like written notices or filings."
- **(B) Ground truth calibration**: Review whether the ground truth is too narrow — some of the "extra" items the LLM finds (e.g., filing contracts with county) could reasonably be classified as record-keeping.

### Priority 3: Q-G3 Minor Omission

**Problem:** Missing "owner files contract changes within 10 business days." Answer is otherwise comprehensive (2/3).

**Root cause:** Either the specific chunk containing this clause is ranked low in PPR, or the synthesis LLM deprioritizes it. This is a minor gap — the answer covers all major financial obligations exhaustively.

**Potential fix:** Low priority. Could increase `ROUTE7_PPR_PASSAGE_TOP_K` from 20 to 30 to widen the retrieval funnel, or enable `ROUTE7_SENTENCE_SEARCH=1` to add parallel sentence-level retrieval.

### Priority 4: Route 7 Architectural Limitations for Thematic Queries

**Background:** Q-G4's retrieval gap (PMA monthly statement was entirely missing in v2) exposed Route 7's structural weakness for thematic/enumerative queries without strong named-entity anchors. The per-document entity-doc map helped (PMA chunks now surface in v3), but Route 7's PPR-based architecture fundamentally favors entity-anchored queries over "list all X across documents" queries.

**Options for structural improvement:**
- **(A) Enable `ROUTE7_SENTENCE_SEARCH=1`**: Parallel sentence-level vector search bypasses PPR entirely and can independently find thematic mentions like "monthly statement of income and expenses"
- **(B) Enable `ROUTE7_COMMUNITY_SEEDS=1`**: Community entity seeds help PPR reach more entity clusters
- **(C) Inject community summaries into synthesis context**: Currently community summaries are only used for entity seeding (when enabled); passing them as context text would give the LLM thematic coverage similar to Route 6
- **(D) Router improvement**: Route thematic questions to Route 6 (concept search) instead of forcing Route 7
